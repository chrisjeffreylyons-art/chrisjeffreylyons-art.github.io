About My Musical Side

Music has been central to my life long before marketing was. From competing on a 5A-level snare drum line to advancing through BYU's piano grades in record time, music taught me the discipline, precision, and creative confidence I carry into every marketing campaign.

I volunteer as a piano instructor at the Dixon Resource Center, and during my time as a volunteer representative in Everett, WA, I used piano lessons and music to help over 500 Ukrainian and Russian immigrants integrate culturally into their new communities.

Music isn't just a hobby — it's how I connect with people on a human level. That same instinct drives everything I do as a digital marketer: finding the rhythm in data, the melody in a brand story, and the beat that makes people stop scrolling.

My Musical Journey

A timeline of milestones — each one shaping who I am today.

  1. Piano
    • Advanced from Grade 8 to Grade 9 in just three months under BYU Piano Performance faculty
    • Volunteer Piano Instructor at the Dixon Resource Center, Provo UT
    • Taught piano and English to 500+ Ukrainian and Russian immigrants in Everett, WA
    • Accompanying and composing original pieces as a solo and collaborative pianist
  2. Percussion
    • Timber Creek Snare Drum Line — Indoor & Outdoor competitive circuits
    • Placed Top 3 in 8+ statewide competitions at the 5A level
    • High-pressure ensemble performance builds the same instincts as managing live campaigns
  3. Composition & Performance
    • Composing original works as both pianist and percussionist
    • Covering and arranging music across classical, contemporary, and pop styles
    • Accompanying vocalists and ensembles in live performance settings
